How Much Does a Bathroom Renovation Cost in 2026?

Updated May 2026 · 7 min read

A bathroom renovation is one of the most impactful home improvements you can make — and one of the most expensive per square foot. In 2026, a mid-range bathroom remodel costs between $10,000 and $25,000, while a basic cosmetic update can be done for $3,000–$8,000.

What Drives Bathroom Renovation Costs?

Tile is the biggest variable. Basic ceramic tile runs $1–$3 per sq ft; natural stone or large-format tiles can cost $10–$30 per sq ft, plus higher installation costs. Tiling a standard 5×8 bathroom floor and shower surround typically costs $2,000–$6,000 in materials and labor.

Labor accounts for 40–60% of total cost. Plumbing and electrical work are the most expensive trades involved. Any time you move a drain, toilet, or add new circuits, costs jump significantly.

Fixture choices matter. A basic toilet costs $150–$300; a wall-hung model runs $600–$1,200. A standard vanity is $300–$800; custom cabinetry can reach $3,000+. These choices add up fast in a small space.

Bathroom Renovation ROI

According to the 2025/2026 Cost vs. Value Report, a mid-range bathroom remodel returns about 74% of its cost at resale. An upscale remodel returns roughly 57%. This makes mid-range updates the better financial choice for most homeowners.

The best ROI comes from refreshing a dated bathroom rather than creating a luxury spa. Buyers want functional, clean, and updated — not necessarily extravagant.

How to Budget a Bathroom Renovation

Start with your total budget and work backward. Allocate roughly 40% to labor, 25% to fixtures, 20% to tile and materials, and keep 15% as a contingency. Unexpected issues — rotted subfloor, outdated plumbing, mold — are common in older bathrooms.

Get at least 3 contractor quotes. Bathroom renovation prices vary by 30–50% between contractors in the same market. A detailed scope of work helps you compare quotes accurately.

Cost-Saving Tips for Bathroom Remodels

  • Keep plumbing in the same location — moving drains can add $1,000–$3,000 to the job
  • Use large-format tile on floors and walls — fewer grout lines mean less maintenance and a cleaner look
  • Resurface an existing tub instead of replacing it ($300–$600 vs. $1,000–$3,000)
  • Shop fixture sales at Home Depot, Lowe's, and Ferguson Bath Kitchen & Lighting
  • Paint existing cabinets and replace hardware instead of replacing the vanity
