How to Calculate Flooring for Any Room (Step-by-Step)

Updated May 2026 · 6 min read

Buying too little flooring means a second trip mid-project. Buying too much wastes money. Here's exactly how to calculate flooring for any room — with waste factors, box conversions, and tips for odd-shaped spaces.

Step 1: Measure the Room

Measure the length and width of the room at its widest points. If the room has a closet, measure it separately and add it to the total. For L-shaped rooms, divide the space into two rectangles and calculate each one.

Multiply length × width to get your base square footage. Example: 12 ft × 10 ft = 120 sq ft.

Step 2: Add Your Waste Factor

Never buy exactly the amount you need — waste is inevitable from cuts, breaks, and imperfect boards. Add:

  • 5% — Simple rectangular rooms, straight install
  • 10% — Standard recommendation for most rooms
  • 15% — Diagonal installation, L-shaped rooms
  • 20% — Herringbone, chevron, or complex patterns

At 10% waste: 120 sq ft × 1.10 = 132 sq ft to purchase.

Step 3: Convert to Boxes

Most flooring is sold in boxes that cover a specific square footage. Check the box label — it'll say something like "covers 20.5 sq ft per box." Divide your total sq ft by the box coverage and round up to the nearest whole box.

Example: 132 sq ft ÷ 20.5 sq ft/box = 6.4 → buy 7 boxes.

Pro Tips for Buying Flooring

  • Buy all flooring from the same batch/lot number — colors vary slightly between production runs
  • Let hardwood and LVP acclimate in the room for 24–72 hours before installation
  • Keep a box of leftover flooring for future repairs — matching discontinued products is nearly impossible
  • Check return policies before buying — most stores accept unopened boxes
