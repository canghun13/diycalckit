How Much Does It Cost to Paint a Room in 2026?

Updated May 2026 · 6 min read

Painting a room is one of the most cost-effective home improvements you can make — but costs vary more than most homeowners expect. In 2026, the average cost to paint a single room ranges from $380 to $790 when hiring a professional painter, or as little as $80–$150 in materials if you do it yourself.

What Affects the Cost to Paint a Room?

Room size is the biggest cost driver. Painters typically charge $1.50–$4.00 per square foot of wall area. A larger room simply means more wall area, more paint, and more time.

Ceiling height matters too. Standard 8-ft ceilings are easy to reach with a standard roller extension. Rooms with 10-ft or vaulted ceilings require ladders and extra time, adding $50–$150 to the job.

Paint quality has a big impact on material cost. Budget paint runs $20–$35 per gallon, while premium paints (Benjamin Moore Aura, Sherwin-Williams Emerald) cost $70–$90 per gallon. Premium paints typically cover better and last longer, reducing the number of coats needed.

Number of coats affects both time and material cost. A standard repaint with a similar color usually needs 2 coats. Going from dark to light can require 3 or more coats, increasing both paint cost and labor time.

Professional Painter vs. DIY: Is It Worth Hiring?

DIY painting saves 60–75% on cost but requires time, effort, and skill. A 12×12 room that a pro can paint in 4–6 hours might take a first-timer a full weekend, including prep, painting, and cleanup.

Hiring a pro makes sense for: rooms with high ceilings, extensive trim work, textured walls, or when you need the job done quickly and cleanly. DIY is perfectly viable for standard rooms where prep work is minimal.

How to Calculate How Much Paint You Need

Before buying paint, calculate wall area: add up the room perimeter, multiply by ceiling height, and subtract 21 sq ft per door and 15 sq ft per window. Divide by 350 (sq ft per gallon) and multiply by your number of coats.

Tips to Save on Painting Costs

  • Do the prep yourself (patching holes, taping trim) and hire a pro just for painting
  • Buy paint during sales at Home Depot or Lowe's — seasonal discounts of 25–40% are common
  • Stick to one accent wall instead of painting all four walls a bold color
  • Get quotes from at least 3 painters — prices vary by 30–50% in most markets
  • Avoid painting in humid weather — it slows drying and can cause paint to run
