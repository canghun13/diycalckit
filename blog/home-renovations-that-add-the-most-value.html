10 Home Renovations That Add the Most Value in 2026

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Not all home improvements are created equal. Some renovations return nearly every dollar spent at resale; others barely make a dent. Based on the 2025/2026 Remodeling Magazine Cost vs. Value Report, here are the 10 projects with the highest return on investment.

1. Garage Door Replacement — 194% ROI

The single best-returning home improvement in 2026. A new insulated garage door costs roughly $4,000–$4,500 installed and adds an estimated $8,000+ in home value. It's highly visible, signals maintenance, and buyers notice it immediately.

2. Steel Entry Door Replacement — 188% ROI

A new steel entry door ($2,400 average) returns nearly double its cost. It boosts curb appeal, improves energy efficiency, and enhances security — three things buyers care about.

3. Manufactured Stone Veneer — 153% ROI

Adding stone veneer to the front facade (average cost: $11,000) returns over 150% of cost. It dramatically improves curb appeal and makes even modest homes look premium.

4. Minor Kitchen Remodel — 96% ROI

A minor kitchen update — new cabinet fronts, countertops, appliances, and paint — costs about $27,000 on average and returns 96 cents per dollar at resale. Far better than a major gut renovation at 62% ROI.

5. Wood Deck Addition — 83% ROI

Outdoor living space is consistently high-value. A wood deck addition ($17,000 average) returns 83% of cost and adds usable square footage buyers can see and enjoy immediately.

6. Vinyl Siding Replacement — 80% ROI

New siding ($17,000 average) signals a well-maintained home and returns 80% of cost. Buyers factor exterior condition heavily into their offer price.

7. Composite Deck Addition — 68% ROI

Composite decking costs more than wood upfront ($24,000 average) but requires less maintenance. At 68% ROI, it's slightly lower than wood but offers longer-term value.

8. Vinyl Window Replacement — 69% ROI

New windows ($20,000 for 10 windows) are valued by buyers for energy efficiency and reduced maintenance. They return 69% on average, with higher returns in colder climates.

9. Bathroom Remodel (Mid-Range) — 74% ROI

A full mid-range bathroom remodel ($25,000) returns 74% at resale. Bathrooms and kitchens are the rooms buyers scrutinize most, so outdated baths hurt sale prices significantly.

10. Backup Power Generator — 95% ROI

A whole-house standby generator ($18,000–$24,000 installed) returns about 95% of its cost — and has become increasingly valuable as extreme weather events drive demand.

What Not to Renovate Before Selling

Swimming pools (50% ROI), sunroom additions (52%), and major upscale kitchen remodels in mid-range neighborhoods consistently underperform. Don't over-improve beyond the neighborhood standard — buyers compare to comparable sales, not your taste.
