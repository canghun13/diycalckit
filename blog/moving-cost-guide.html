How Much Does It Cost to Move in 2026? Complete Guide

Updated May 2026 · 7 min read

Moving costs vary enormously depending on home size, distance, and how much help you hire. In 2026, a local move for a 2-bedroom home averages $800–$2,500. A cross-country move for the same home runs $4,000–$10,000.

What's Included in Moving Costs?

Labor is the biggest cost — movers typically charge $100–$200 per hour for a 2-man crew. Most local moves take 4–8 hours. Long-distance moves are often priced by weight and distance rather than hourly.

Truck rental for a DIY move costs $30–$150 per day plus mileage ($0.99–$1.29/mile for long distance). For cross-country moves, truck rental often ends up cheaper than full-service moving if you're willing to do the work.

Packing materials — boxes, tape, packing paper — add $100–$400 depending on home size. Buying used boxes on Facebook Marketplace or getting free boxes from liquor stores and bookstores can cut this in half.

Full Service vs. Labor Only vs. DIY

Full service (movers pack and move everything) costs 35–45% more than labor-only but saves significant time and stress. Best for long-distance moves or when time is tight.

Labor only (you pack, they move) is the most popular option for local moves. You save on packing labor and have full control over how fragile items are packed.

DIY rental truck is the cheapest option for local moves but the hardest physically. For cross-country moves, truck rental costs can approach full-service pricing once you factor in fuel, hotels, and your time.

Tips to Save on Moving Costs

  • Move mid-week and mid-month — Weekends and month-end dates are 15–25% more expensive due to lease cycles
  • Book early — Summer moves (May–September) book up fast; 6–8 weeks advance booking is recommended
  • Declutter before the move — Every pound you don't move saves money on long-distance pricing
  • Get at least 3 quotes — Prices vary 30–50% between moving companies in the same market
  • Check for liability coverage — Basic moving coverage is 60 cents per pound; buy additional coverage for valuable items
