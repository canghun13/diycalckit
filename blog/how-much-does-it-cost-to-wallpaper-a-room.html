How Much Does It Cost to Wallpaper a Room in 2026?

Updated June 2026 · 7 min read

Wallpaper has made a real comeback — from bold accent walls to full-room applications in living rooms and bedrooms. But before committing to a pattern, it helps to know the full cost. For a standard 12×12 bedroom, expect to spend $300–$800 in wallpaper materials and another $300–$800 for professional installation. Total project costs of $600–$1,600 are typical for a single room; larger rooms or premium wallpaper push that higher. Here's what drives the price.

Wallpaper Cost by Type

The wallpaper itself is the biggest variable in your budget. Prices below are per double roll (US standard), which covers approximately 25–30 usable square feet after accounting for pattern waste.

For most homeowners doing a single bedroom, mid-range non-woven wallpaper at $60–$100 per roll is the sweet spot — durable, forgiving to hang, and strippable when you're ready for a change. Peel-and-stick has improved significantly but still doesn't last as long as traditional paste wallpaper in humid environments.

Professional Installation Cost

Labor for wallpaper installation runs $1.50–$4.00 per square foot of wall area, or roughly $300–$800 for a standard 12×12 bedroom. Several factors push the price higher:

  • Pattern complexity. Large repeats require careful alignment at every seam and take significantly longer. Expect a 20–40% labor premium for patterns with repeats over 12 inches.
  • Wall preparation. Installers charge extra for wall repairs, skim coating over textured walls, or removing old wallpaper. Old wallpaper removal alone can add $1–$2 per square foot to the job.
  • Ceilings. Ceiling installation is more labor-intensive than walls — expect to pay 50–100% more per square foot for ceiling work.
  • Wallpaper type. Grasscloth and natural fibers are harder to work with and cost more to install. Paste-the-wall non-woven is the most installer-friendly type.

Get at least two quotes before hiring. Local wallpaper installers (found through paint stores or Houzz) typically charge less than full-service interior decorators who manage the project end-to-end.

DIY works well for peel-and-stick and basic vinyl with no or small pattern repeat. For expensive grasscloth or large-repeat designer wallpaper, professional installation is worth the cost — mistakes on a $150/roll pattern are expensive. One misaligned seam or bubbled panel on a $1,500 wallpaper job can ruin the whole effect.

Hidden Costs to Budget For

Beyond wallpaper and labor, a few line items catch homeowners off guard. Wallpaper primer or sizing agent runs $15–$40 per gallon and is essential for proper adhesion and future strippability — skipping it is a false economy. Seam tape, paste (if not pre-pasted), and a plastic smoother add another $20–$40. If your walls have texture, skim coating to get a smooth surface adds $1.50–$3.00 per square foot in labor. Finally, old wallpaper removal — if you're replacing existing paper — typically runs $1–$2 per square foot and should be quoted separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rolls of wallpaper do I need for a 12×12 room? A 12×12 room with 8-ft ceilings, one door, and two windows needs approximately 10–12 rolls for a medium pattern repeat (6–12 inches). Use the wallpaper calculator for your exact dimensions.

Is peel-and-stick wallpaper cheaper? Per roll, peel-and-stick is generally cheaper — but it covers less area and doesn't last as long. For permanent installations, traditional paste wallpaper is more cost-effective over time.

Does wallpaper increase home value? Tasteful, neutral wallpaper in good condition is generally viewed positively by buyers. Bold patterns are more divisive and may require removal before listing. Accent walls are the safest investment — they add visual interest without overwhelming the room.
