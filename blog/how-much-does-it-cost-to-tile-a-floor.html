How Much Does It Cost to Tile a Floor in 2026?

Updated May 2026 · 6 min read

Tile flooring costs between $7 and $25 per square foot installed in 2026, including materials and labor. For a standard 10×10 bathroom floor (100 sq ft), expect to pay $700–$2,500 total.

What Affects Tile Installation Cost?

Tile size and pattern significantly impact labor cost. Large-format tiles (18×18 or larger) require more careful leveling and take longer to set. Diagonal or herringbone patterns add 15–25% to labor because of additional cuts.

Subfloor condition is a hidden cost driver. If the existing subfloor is uneven, cracked, or needs waterproofing membrane (for bathrooms), expect to add $2–$5 per sq ft before any tile goes down.

Grout and setting materials add $1–$3 per sq ft. Epoxy grout costs more than standard cement grout but is stain-resistant and lasts longer in kitchens and bathrooms.

DIY vs. Hiring a Tile Setter

Tiling is a DIY-friendly project for simple rooms with straight walls. Labor typically runs $4–$9 per sq ft, so a 100 sq ft bathroom floor saves $400–$900 by doing it yourself. That said, a bad tile job is expensive to fix — uneven tiles, poor grout lines, and improper waterproofing are common DIY mistakes.

How to Calculate Tiles Needed

Divide your room's square footage by the tile size (in sq ft) and add 10–15% for waste. A 10×10 room needs about 111 tiles of 12×12 tiles at 10% waste.
