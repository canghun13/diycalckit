How to Calculate Drywall Sheets

Calculate total wall area by multiplying perimeter by ceiling height. Subtract 21 sq ft per door and 15 sq ft per window. Divide by the sheet area (32 sq ft for 4×8 sheets) and add 10% for waste.

Standard drywall sheets are 4×8 ft (32 sq ft). For rooms with 9-ft ceilings, 4×12 sheets reduce seams and save time on finishing.

Standard ½" drywall is the right choice for most wall and ceiling applications. Use moisture-resistant board (green or purple board) in bathrooms and near any water source — standard drywall behind tile will absorb moisture and eventually fail. Garages attached to living spaces typically require ⅝" Type X fire-resistant board by code.

Drywall Finishing Costs

Material cost is only part of drywall work. Finishing (taping, mudding, and sanding) can be as time-consuming as hanging. Professional drywall finishing runs $1.50–$3.00 per square foot of finished surface. A typical 12×12 room with 8-ft ceilings has about 550 sq ft of wall and ceiling area — finishing costs $825–$1,650 in labor.

DIY drywall finishing is achievable but requires practice. The three-coat process (tape coat, fill coat, finish coat) takes 3–5 days for a standard room due to drying time between coats. Feathering the edges smoothly for an invisible seam is the hardest skill — budget for practice on a small area first.

Pro tip: Buy drywall in 4×12 sheets for rooms with standard 8-ft ceilings — you'll cover the entire height in one sheet and have fewer horizontal seams to tape.

Common Drywall Ordering Mistakes

  • Using 4×8 sheet count when 4×12 sheets are available — Longer sheets mean fewer seams to tape and finish, which saves real time and mud on larger walls. Check what your local supplier stocks before finalizing a sheet count.
  • Forgetting the ceiling — Ceiling drywall is easy to leave out of a room-by-room takeoff since it's not a "wall." Measure it as its own area and add it to the total.
  • Not accounting for moisture-resistant board where needed — Bathrooms and areas near plumbing need green board or moisture-resistant drywall, which is priced and ordered separately from standard board — mixing this up mid-project causes delays.
  • Underestimating joint compound — A rule of thumb is one gallon of pre-mixed mud per 100 sq ft of drywall for a basic 2-3 coat finish — running short mid-taping means a delay while a fresh batch dries to match.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many drywall sheets do I need for a 12x12 room?

A 12x12 room with 8-ft walls has about 320 sq ft of wall area. A standard 4x8 sheet covers 32 sq ft, so plan on roughly 10 sheets before waste, or about 11 with a 10% buffer.

Should I use 1/2 inch or 5/8 inch drywall?

Use 1/2 inch for standard interior walls and ceilings. Use 5/8 inch (Type X, fire-rated) for garages and any fire-rated assembly required by code.

How much joint compound do I need?

Plan on roughly 1 gallon of joint compound per 100 sq ft of drywall across all coats combined (tape, fill, and finish).

Do I need extra drywall for the ceiling?

Yes. Calculate ceiling area (length x width) separately from wall area and add it to your total sheet count.
