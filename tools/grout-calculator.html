How to Calculate Grout Needed

The amount of grout depends on tile size, joint width, tile thickness, and grout type density. The industry-standard formula from the TCNA Handbook calculates total joint volume, converts it to pounds using grout-specific density, adds a 10% waste buffer, then divides by your bag size for an exact count.

Key insight: Joint width has far more impact than tile size. A ¼" joint uses roughly twice as much grout as a ⅛" joint on the same tile. Always buy at least 10% extra — grout is sold in production batches, and color can vary between batches.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much grout do I need for 100 sq ft of tile?

For a 12x12 inch tile with a 1/8 inch joint, expect roughly 2-3 bags (25 lb each) of sanded grout per 100 sq ft — smaller tiles or wider joints need significantly more.

What's the difference between sanded and unsanded grout?

Sanded grout is for joints 1/8 inch and wider. Unsanded is for joints narrower than 1/8 inch and for polished stone or glass tile, which sanded grout can scratch.

How does joint width affect grout quantity?

Wider joints use significantly more grout — going from a 1/8 inch to a 1/4 inch joint can roughly double the amount needed for the same tile area.

Should I buy extra grout for a custom color?

Yes, always buy one extra bag. Custom or discontinued colors can vary between production batches, so running short mid-project risks a visible mismatch.
