How to Calculate Square Footage

Multiply length × width for each rectangular room and add the totals together. For odd-shaped rooms, divide them into rectangles, calculate each section, and sum the results. For L-shaped rooms, split at the inside corner into two rectangles.

Always measure at the widest points of each room. Don't subtract for furniture, islands, or built-ins — flooring and renovation materials run underneath or around these. Do measure closets separately and add them in; a standard reach-in closet adds 8–12 sq ft, a walk-in adds 25–60 sq ft.

What to Use Square Footage For

Knowing your room or home square footage is essential for buying flooring (sq ft + waste %), ordering paint (wall area calculation), sizing HVAC equipment, estimating renovation costs, and calculating property value comparisons. Most material calculators start with square footage as the base input.

For real estate purposes, finished square footage typically excludes garages, unfinished basements, and spaces with ceilings under 7 feet. For material purchasing, measure every area the material will cover — including under furniture and inside closets.

Pro tip: When calculating for flooring, measure each room separately and account for closets and hallways individually. Add rooms together at the end, then apply your waste factor to the total.
