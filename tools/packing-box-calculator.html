How Many Boxes Do I Need to Move?

The number of moving boxes depends on the size of your home and how much stuff you own. On average, a 1-bedroom apartment needs 20–30 boxes, a 2-bedroom home needs 30–50 boxes, and a 3-bedroom home needs 50–80 boxes.

Kitchens and bedrooms are the most box-intensive rooms. Don't forget to account for closets, garages, and storage areas — these often need more boxes than expected.

Never pack heavy items in large boxes — a large box full of books can weigh 80+ lbs and is a back injury waiting to happen. Use small boxes for heavy items (books, records, tools, canned food) and larger boxes for light items that take up space (linens, pillows, decorative items).

Where to Get Free Moving Boxes

Buying all new boxes adds up fast — a full pack of 20–30 boxes costs $60–$150 at moving supply stores. Free alternatives include: liquor stores (small, sturdy boxes perfect for books), bookstores, Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace ("free moving boxes" is a frequent listing), Nextdoor app, Buy Nothing groups, and behind grocery stores. Ask a week before your move and check the day of — stores accumulate boxes quickly.

Pro tip: Order boxes from Home Depot or U-Haul a week before moving. Buying used boxes from Facebook Marketplace can cut costs by 50%.

Common Packing Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overpacking boxes — A box packed to the brim with dense items is a common cause of blown-out bottoms mid-move. Keep any single box under about 50 lbs — if it's too heavy to lift comfortably, split it into two.
  • Leaving boxes half-empty — Underfilled boxes collapse when stacked. Fill empty space with towels, clothing, or packing paper so the box holds its shape.
  • Skipping labels — Label every box with both the destination room and a general contents summary. "Kitchen — pots/pans" gets unpacked correctly on move-in day; an unlabeled box gets opened three times before anyone finds what's inside.
  • Packing plates flat — Stack plates vertically, on their edge, like records — not flat in a stack. Vertical plates absorb shock far better and break far less often in transit.
  • No "open first" box — Pack one clearly marked box with toiletries, chargers, a change of clothes, and basic tools. You'll want it within the first hour at the new place, not buried under 60 other boxes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many boxes do I need to move a 3-bedroom house?

A typical 3-bedroom home needs 60-80 boxes total, including small, medium, large, and specialty boxes like dish packs and wardrobe boxes.

What size boxes should I use for books?

Always use small boxes for books and other dense items — a large box full of books becomes too heavy to lift safely.

How many boxes does a typical kitchen need?

A standard kitchen typically needs 10-15 boxes, including several dish packs for fragile items and medium boxes for pantry goods.

Should I buy new boxes or find free ones?

Free boxes work fine for books and non-fragile items, but dish packs and wardrobe boxes are worth buying new since they're purpose-built for fragile or hanging items.
