How Many Boxes Do I Need to Move?

The number of moving boxes depends on the size of your home and how much stuff you own. On average, a 1-bedroom apartment needs 20–30 boxes, a 2-bedroom home needs 30–50 boxes, and a 3-bedroom home needs 50–80 boxes.

Kitchens and bedrooms are the most box-intensive rooms. Don't forget to account for closets, garages, and storage areas — these often need more boxes than expected.

Standard Box Sizes and What to Pack in Each

Box SizeDimensionsBest For
Small (1.5 cu ft)16×12×12"Books, canned food, tools, heavy items
Medium (3 cu ft)18×18×16"Kitchen items, toys, small appliances
Large (4.5 cu ft)18×18×24"Pillows, linens, lamps, light décor
Extra Large (6 cu ft)24×24×18"Comforters, coats, oversized items
Wardrobe (15+ cu ft)24×20×45"Hanging clothes (keeps them wrinkle-free)

Never pack heavy items in large boxes — a large box full of books can weigh 80+ lbs and is a back injury waiting to happen. Use small boxes for heavy items (books, records, tools, canned food) and larger boxes for light items that take up space (linens, pillows, decorative items).

Where to Get Free Moving Boxes

Buying all new boxes adds up fast — a full pack of 20–30 boxes costs $60–$150 at moving supply stores. Free alternatives include: liquor stores (small, sturdy boxes perfect for books), bookstores, Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace ("free moving boxes" is a frequent listing), Nextdoor app, Buy Nothing groups, and behind grocery stores. Ask a week before your move and check the day of — stores accumulate boxes quickly.

Pro tip: Order boxes from Home Depot or U-Haul a week before moving. Buying used boxes from Facebook Marketplace can cut costs by 50%.
