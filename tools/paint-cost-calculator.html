What's Included in This Estimate

Paint: Calculated at 350 sq ft per gallon per coat. The calculator accounts for wall area minus doors and windows, plus ceiling if selected.

Primer: One coat at 300 sq ft per gallon, budget grade (~$22/gal). Only added when selected — skip it if you're repainting the same color over sound existing paint.

Supplies: Roller covers, painter's tape, drop cloth, tray, and brushes. Estimated at a flat $40–$60 for a typical room. Reusable supplies are cheaper if you already have them.

Labor: Professional painters charge $2–$4 per sq ft for walls only, or $3–$6 per sq ft including ceilings and trim. The estimate uses the midpoint of current 2026 rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint a 12x12 room?

Materials alone typically run $80-$150. Adding professional labor brings the total to $300-$800 depending on your region and prep work needed.

How much does professional painting labor cost per square foot?

Professional interior painting labor typically runs $2-$6 per square foot of wall area, varying by region and wall condition.

Does paint quality affect overall project cost significantly?

Premium paint costs $15-25 more per gallon, but often needs one fewer coat, so the total cost gap is smaller than the per-gallon price difference suggests.

Is it cheaper to paint a room myself or hire a professional?

DIY painting saves 70-85% of total cost since labor is the largest expense, but professional results may be worth it for high ceilings, trim work, or tight timelines.
