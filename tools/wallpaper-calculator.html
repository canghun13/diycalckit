How to Calculate Wallpaper Rolls

Calculate total wall area, subtract doors and windows, and divide by the usable area per roll. Pattern repeats waste 15–35% of each strip, so account for that when calculating strips per roll.

Wallpaper Cost and What to Expect

Wallpaper costs vary widely — from $30 per roll for basic vinyl to $150+ for designer or grasscloth options. Most rolls cover 25–30 square feet after accounting for pattern repeat and waste. Budget wallpaper projects for a standard 12×12 bedroom typically run $200–$500 in materials, while premium wallpaper in the same room can easily reach $1,000–$2,000.

Professional installation adds $1.50–$4.00 per square foot in labor, or $300–$800 per room for most standard bedrooms. Complex patterns with large repeats require more material and take longer to align, increasing both material waste and labor cost.

Wallpaper Hanging Tips

Always buy extra rolls — the same wallpaper pattern can vary slightly between print batches (called dye lots). Buy all rolls from the same batch and keep one spare roll for future repairs. Returning unopened rolls is usually possible, but matching a discontinued batch later is not.

Before hanging, prepare the wall: patch any holes, sand smooth, and apply a wallpaper primer or sizing agent. Hanging on unprimed drywall makes future removal extremely difficult, as the paper bonds directly to the drywall facing. A $15 gallon of wallpaper primer is essential preparation.

Pro tip: Always buy extra rolls from the same batch number. Wallpaper color can vary slightly between print runs.
