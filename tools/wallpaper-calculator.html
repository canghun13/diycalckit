How to Calculate Wallpaper Rolls

Calculate total wall area by adding the perimeter of the room (length + width × 2) and multiplying by ceiling height. Subtract doors (roughly 21 sq ft each) and windows (roughly 15 sq ft each). Divide the net wall area by the usable square footage per roll, accounting for pattern repeat waste. Always round up and add at least one extra roll.

The formula: (Perimeter × Height − Doors − Windows) ÷ Usable sq ft per roll = Rolls needed

Pattern repeat waste matters more than most homeowners expect. A large repeat (over 12 inches) wastes 30–35% of each strip to align the pattern, which means you need significantly more rolls than the raw wall area suggests. The calculator accounts for this automatically.

Wallpaper Buying Tips

Always buy all rolls from the same dye lot (batch number printed on the label). Wallpaper color can vary slightly between production runs, and replacing a roll from a different batch mid-project will show a visible line. Most retailers allow returns of unopened rolls, so it's worth buying one extra.

Before hanging, prep the wall: patch any holes, sand smooth, and apply a wallpaper primer or sizing agent. Skipping this step — especially on new drywall — bonds the paper directly to the drywall facing, making future removal extremely destructive. A $15 gallon of wallpaper primer is the most important prep investment you can make.

Pro tip: For an accent wall only, divide the wall's square footage by your roll's usable coverage. A standard 10-ft accent wall at 8-ft ceiling height is 80 sq ft — usually 3–4 rolls depending on pattern repeat.
