What Drives Bathroom Remodel Costs?

Labor is the biggest line item. For a professionally contracted bathroom remodel, labor typically makes up 40–65% of the total cost. A tile setter, plumber, and electrician each have minimum charges, and small bathrooms don't cost much less in labor than larger ones for the same scope of work.

Moving plumbing adds significant cost. If your remodel keeps the toilet, tub, and shower in their current positions, costs stay manageable. Moving a toilet requires a licensed plumber and often means cutting into the subfloor — add $500–$2,000 or more depending on the complexity. Moving a drain is one of the most common sources of budget overruns in bathroom renovations.

Tile choice affects both material and labor cost. A simple 12×12 grid of ceramic tile installs faster and with less waste than a herringbone pattern in 3×6 subway tile. The tile itself may cost $2/sq ft vs $5/sq ft — but the labor difference between a straight lay and a complex pattern can be $3–$8 per square foot. Pattern complexity is where bathroom budgets quietly expand.

Waterproofing is mandatory in wet areas. A proper shower installation requires a waterproofing membrane (like Schluter Kerdi or RedGard) before any tile goes up. This adds $400–$750 to the project and is non-negotiable if you want the shower to last. Contractors who quote shower work without mentioning waterproofing are either including it silently or skipping it — always ask explicitly.

How to Get the Most Accurate Estimate

Calculator estimates give you a planning range, not a quote. The only way to get an accurate number is to get at least three itemized contractor quotes for your specific bathroom. When comparing quotes, make sure each one includes the same scope — demo, waterproofing, tile installation, fixtures, and cleanup. A low quote that excludes waterproofing or disposal isn't a bargain.

Always build a 15–20% contingency into your budget before starting. Bathroom renovations routinely uncover hidden issues once walls are opened: rotted subfloor, outdated plumbing connections, missing waterproofing on an existing shower, or electrical that doesn't meet current code. These aren't rare surprises — they're common enough that professionals expect them.

Pro tip: Get quotes from at least three licensed contractors. Labor costs for the same scope can vary 30–50% between contractors in the same market — comparison shopping is the single most effective way to control bathroom remodel costs.
